Anecdotes

Oppenheimer returned to the U.S. and took assistant professor positions at California Institute of Technology and Berkeley University. The annual salary at the time was $3,300, the equivalent of $75,000 today. At Berkeley, his classroom was next to Lawrence's lab. Lawrence was building the world's first high-energy particle cyclotron (Cyclotron). This work earned him the 1939 Nobel Prize in Physics.

The two had heard of each other when they first met. After exchanging pleasantries, Lawrence asked Oppenheimer are you here to lecture or teach? Oppenheimer said he was lecturing, on quantum mechanics, but there was only one student. This student was the later American physicist Giovanni Ross. Giovanni Roth. Giovanni Rossi Lomanitz. Later, Lawrence noticed that there was a lot of left-wing activity in Oppenheimer's classroom, so he warned Oppenheimer to remove these political groups from the classroom.

The way to success

When the newspapers reported that German scientists had split a uranium nucleus with a neutron bombardment, Oppenheimer was horrified and said, "That can't be right" after doing some calculations on the blackboard. Lawrence laughed and told him that a young researcher, Alvarez, had replicated the experiment in a nearby laboratory! Luis Alvarez. Luis Walter Alvarez later joined the Manhattan Project. He was awarded the 1968 Nobel Prize in Physics in 1968 for his decisive contributions to elementary particle physics, in particular for his development of the hydrogen bubble chamber technique and the method of analyzing the data, which led to the discovery of a large number of resonance states.
